Managing Procrastination

We all have that project, homework, assignment, whatever - that is simply so boring or daunting that we'd rather do anything else. Yeah, we all say that we can manage doing it before the day of the deadline, but of course that never happens. Here's how you manage procrastination:

Anish's Guide to Managing Procrastination:

  • Motivate Ask yourself, what will happen if you submit this task
  • Divide Break down the task at hand to subparts.
  • Plan Write the subparts down on a piece of paper.
  • Plan #2 Write down the steps required to solve those subparts.
  • Conquer Do the tasks you have listed and cross them out one by one.

Scheduling

Scheduling is an important thing that most of us never think to do, because, let's be honest, it's kind of boring and repititive. I mean, who wants to make their schedule everyday? However, allocating time to every one of your responsibilities throughout the day has made me more accountable to myself, and therefore more productive.

Creating A Schedule that's Simple

Everyone can make a schedule, right? It's that kinda thing that you think that you can do, but it never works. Expectations never really match reality. You might have some sort of important meeting that crops up during some other activity that you wanted to do, and now the schedule goes out of the window. I will describe a schedule creation method that welcomes distractions.

Materials Needed: A Calendar, A piece of paper
  • Make 2 columns named Today, Later
  • Beginning of Day: Take 5 minutes to fill out what you need to do that day in this format: Time - Thing
  • As you go throughout the day, keep crossing events off the list
  • As you encounter new events, schedule them into the Later column
  • If you have to override a scheduled event, jot the scheduled event in the Later column.
  • End of Day: Put the events in the Later column in the Calendar.
